Syunik's Unexplored Destinations

HISTORY

Syunik, a historic region in southern Armenia, was once part of the Kingdom of Armenia. It became an autonomous principality in the 9th century and later a kingdom under the Orbelian dynasty. Conquered by various empires, including the Seljuks and Persians, it eventually integrated into modern Armenia in the 20th century.























CULTURE

Syunik's culture is deeply rooted in Armenian traditions, featuring ancient monasteries, stone khachkars (cross-stones), and a strong connection to nature. The region's people maintain traditional crafts, such as rug weaving and stone carving. Festivals celebrate agricultural cycles, while local cuisine highlights dishes made with fresh, locally sourced ingredients.
